MainCourse Garlic and Herb Buttery Mash [potato, salted butter, warm cream, gralic cloves, parsely, salt, pepper] [Wash and peel potatoes and cut into large chunks. Place in a medium saucepan and just cover with cold water. Bring to the boil then simmer for 20-25 minutes or until fork tender, Drain potatoes and return to the dry pan. Shake pan occasionally to remove excess moisture. Mash the potatoes and stir in ¾ of the butter, Gradually add cream, beating with a wooden spoon until smooth. Stir in garlic and parsley. Season with salt and pepper. Serve topped with remaining butter] salmon
SideDish muffin tin egg pies [sandwich bread, butter, olive oil, onion, carrot, peas, bacon, egg] [Preheat oven to 180°C. Using a rolling pin, roll slices of bread flat. Spread butter over both sides of bread and press into the moulds of a 1 cup-capacity 6-hole Texas muffin pan. Bake for 15 minutes or until golden and crisp. Set aside to cool, Meanwhile, heat the oil in a non-stick frying pan over medium heat. Add onion and carrot and cook for 3 minutes or until softened. Add corn and peas. Remove from heat and stir through ham or bacon. Lightly whisk eggs in a small bowl, then add to vegetable mixture with half the cheese. Season, Spoon vegetable mixture into bread shells. Sprinkle with remaining cheese. Bake for 15 minutes or until egg mixture is set and top of pies are golden] 560
Dessert lemon curd sponge roll [egg, sugar, cornflour, lemon, butter] [Combine eggs, sugar, cornflour, zest and juice in a large microwave-safe bowl. Whisk until combined and smooth. Add the diced butter and place onto the microwave turntable., Follow manufacturer’s instructions to reduce microwave power to 50%. Cook uncovered for 7 – 10 minutes, stirring every minute until mixture is thick enough to coat the back of a wooden spoon., Cover the curd surface with plastic wrap to prevent it from forming a skin. Let cool to room temperature, then refrigerate until cold.] mild
